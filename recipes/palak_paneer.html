Palak Paneer

Description

Palak Paneer is a vibrant and flavorful vegetarian dish from India. It features paneer (cottage cheese) cubes in a creamy spinach gravy, creating a delightful combination of textures and tastes. This recipe is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for a satisfying and healthy meal.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to preference)
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  • 4 cups chopped fresh spinach (or 2 cups frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry)
  • 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked and ground into a paste (optional, for a richer gravy)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (or full-fat coconut milk, for a vegan option)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan or pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the ginger and garlic and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chili pepper (if using) and cook until the tomatoes become mushy, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Stir well and cook for 30 seconds.
  5. Pour in 1/2 cup of water and bring to a simmer. Add salt to taste.
  6. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. If using frozen spinach, add it here and cook until heated through.
  7. (Optional) If using cashew paste, stir it in at this stage and simmer for a further 2-3 minutes.
  8. Gently add the cubed paneer and simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  9. Stir in the heavy cream (or coconut milk) and bring to a gentle simmer. Do not boil.
  10. Adjust seasoning with salt and red chili powder (if needed). Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot with rice or naan.
